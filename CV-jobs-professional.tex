                \item The initial stage of the experiment consisted of a 14-day free-living period, during which diet and activity levels were recorded using a 7-day food diary and a physical activity monitoring bracelet.
                \item The goal was to reduce body weight by 10\% through dietary intake restriction. This was followed by a six-month weight maintenance period.
                \item Basic anthropometric measurements, such as height, weight, waist circumference, and hip circumference, are accepted as baseline data.
                \item Basic metabolic rate (BMR) is measured using a bodpod. Resting metabolic rate (RMR) is measured using a gas metabolism method.
                \item DEXA scanning provides a more precise picture of muscle mass and fat mass distribution. MRI scans are also used to verify the data and to observe changes in the brain before and after weight loss.
                \item Blood glucose and hormone fluctuations were observed before and after meals. A canular in the arm was used to dynamically collect blood for analysis of glucose, GLP-1, and PYY. Video recordings were also used to observe the number of chewing times and eating patterns of volunteers before and after weight loss. An appetite psychology questionnaire was also used to understand eating motivation and appetite.
 
                
                
                \end{highlights} 
                \end{onecolentry}
